---
name: worth-it
description: Use when proposing a new feature. Challenges whether it's worth building by questioning who wants it, the real pain, and what you're giving up.
---

Before I write any code, challenge me on whether this feature should exist at all. Push back on who's asking for it, what happens without it, whether it drives revenue, if I'm over-building, what I'm not building instead, and whether it creates a moat. Work through each point one by one, never moving on until it's resolved. For each question, provide your recommended answer.

Use the AskUserQuestion tool for each question. Explore the codebase, docs, or the web instead of asking me when possible — check competitors, market demand, and whether this problem is already solved.

When done, summarize what we're building, what we cut, and the key assumption we're betting on. Then ask if I want to move on to nah-fam for the technical design.
